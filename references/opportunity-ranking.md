# Opportunity Ranking Rubric

Score each surviving candidate on 10 dimensions (1–5 each, max 50).
Show the score per dimension. Eliminate anything below 25.

## The 10 dimensions

1. **Pain frequency** (1=yearly, 3=weekly, 5=daily)
2. **Emotional intensity** (1=mild annoyance, 3=frustration, 5=fear/burnout)
3. **Financial weight** (1=<$100/mo, 3=$500–2K/mo, 5=>$2K/mo or catastrophic risk)
4. **Workaround intensity** (1=no workaround, 3=personal workaround, 5=team-level or headcount)
5. **Incumbent defensibility** (1=well-solved, 3=partially solved, 5=structurally unserved)
6. **Interview accessibility** (1=no access, 3=warm intro needed, 5=can interview this week)
7. **Distribution feasibility** (1=no clear channel, 3=reachable with effort, 5=active community)
8. **MVP buildability** (1=>6 months, 3=2–4 months, 5=<4 weeks)
9. **Founder-market fit** (1=no connection, 3=adjacent experience, 5=personal experience)
10. **Moat potential** (1=easily cloned, 3=some switching cost, 5=network effects or data moat)

## Score interpretation

| Score | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| 40–50 | Strong candidate — pursue aggressively |
| 30–39 | Worth pursuing — needs 3+ confirming interviews |
| 25–29 | Weak signal — de-prioritize |
| <25   | Eliminate |

## Pressure test questions

- Would the target persona describe this to a stranger at a conference?
- Has anyone built a company around this and failed? Why?
- Is the pain seasonal or structural (every week)?
- Does the problem get worse as the customer grows, or go away?
- Would the customer's boss approve budget to solve this?

## Final founder filter

- Do you find this genuinely interesting to work on for 5 years?
- Do you have enough domain credibility to be taken seriously?
- Is there at least one person you can interview this week?
