# Interview Script Guide

## Full interview structure (30–45 min)

Adapt for each contact type. Replace bracketed prompts with specifics.

### Opening (2 min)
"I'm exploring building software for [industry]. I'm not selling anything —
I'm trying to understand what the actual work looks like. Can I walk through
a recent [week/project] with you?"

### Warm-up (5 min)
- "What did [yesterday/Monday morning] look like? Walk me through it."
- "What's changed about your job in the last year?"
- "If someone new started your role tomorrow with no training, what would
  go wrong first?"

### Workflow mapping (12 min)
For the 2–3 highest-leverage workflows for this role:
- "Walk me through [workflow] from start to finish."
- "What tools at each step? How long does each step take?"
- "What happens when [common disruption] occurs?"
- "What do you do that you wish you didn't have to?"

### Pain probing (8 min)
- "What part of your week do you dread most?"
- "When was the last time [X] caused a real problem — lost money, a customer,
  a complaint? Tell me the full story."
- "What would you do with an extra 5 hours every week?"

### Workaround detection (5 min)
- "What do you track in a spreadsheet that you wish was in your main tool?"
- "What's the most brittle process — the one that breaks when anything
  unexpected happens?"
- "What do you copy-paste every week?"
- "What do you use Slack/email for that should really be in a system?"

### WTP signal (3 min)
- "Has your company ever bought a tool specifically for [problem area]?"
- "If a tool saved you [X hours/week] on [problem], what would you pay?
  What would your manager approve?"

### Closing (2 min)
- "Is there anything I should have asked?"
- "Who else should I talk to — someone in your role at another company,
  or someone with this problem even worse?"

## Post-interview capture (within 1 hour)

1. Single most painful moment (verbatim quote if possible)
2. Current workaround (exactly what they do)
3. Any dollar amounts — time cost, tools they pay for, losses
4. WTP signal — prompted or unprompted?
5. Referrals — who did they mention?
6. Confidence this pain is real (1–5)
