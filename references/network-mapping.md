# Network Mapping Guide

## Interviewability scoring (max 25)

Score each network contact on five dimensions (1–5 each):

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|---|
| **Relationship strength** | Cold/acquaintance | Warm — met a few times | Close — will be honest |
| **Openness about work** | Private/guarded | Will share if asked | Openly complains |
| **Role operationality** | Strategic/executive | Mix strategic + operational | Highly operational |
| **Software spend** | Free tools only | 1–2 paid tools | Multiple paid tools |
| **AI/tech exposure** | Avoids tech | Some exposure | Actively evaluates new tools |

## Niche roles with high operational pain density

Flag these as high-priority when they appear in the founder's network:
- Recruiting coordinator / sourcer
- Property manager (50–500 units)
- Office manager / operations coordinator
- Legal coordinator / paralegal
- Healthcare administrator / care coordinator
- Bookkeeper / accounting specialist (not CFO)
- Field service coordinator / dispatcher
- Event coordinator / production coordinator
- Marketing coordinator / content coordinator
- Insurance coordinator / compliance officer

## Outreach message template

> "Hey [Name], I'm exploring a career change into building software for
> [industry]. I'm not selling anything — I'm trying to understand what the
> actual day-to-day work looks like before I build anything. Would you be up
> for a 30-minute call this week? I'd love to hear about your workflow and
> what drives you crazy about your current tools."

Key principles: lead with curiosity not pitch, specify 30 minutes, make clear
you're not selling, ask about their work not your idea.
