# Incumbent Risk Analysis

## Why incumbents avoid certain problems

1. **ACV too low** — $200/month × 500 customers = $1.2M ARR. Not interesting
   to a $100M revenue company. Very interesting to a founder.
2. **Segment too small or messy** — enterprise vendors define their market
   upward. Small operators have different integrations, buyers, support needs.
3. **Problem is adjacent to core** — Buildium's core is rent and maintenance.
   Vendor compliance is adjacent — real, but not their growth driver.
4. **Platform-locked** — if solving the problem requires integrating with
   competing platforms, the platform vendor won't build it.
5. **Requires specialization** — COI parsing, medical prior-auth, legal
   contract review — incumbents build general tools; specialists win.

## Six questions to answer per candidate

1. Who is the largest current player in this space?
2. Do they serve the same customer segment (size, type)?
3. Could they extend to solve this within 12 months?
4. Do they have a financial incentive to solve it?
5. Is there a structural reason they won't (platform conflict, ACV mismatch)?
6. Are there 2+ incumbents whose customers have this problem — making a
   neutral integration play viable?

## Risk levels

**HIGH** — Incumbent already sells to this exact segment, problem is core to
their value prop, they have a team working on it.

**MEDIUM** — Incumbent could solve this but hasn't prioritized it. No
structural barrier. Watch for downmarket extension.

**LOW** — Incumbent structurally can't or won't serve this segment. Price
point, buyer type, segment size, or platform conflict creates durable barrier.
