# Anti-Pattern Filter

Eliminate any candidate that matches these patterns before scoring.

## The 10 anti-patterns

1. **"AI assistant for X"** — General AI chatbot for a role. Too generic, no
   workflow specificity, no switching cost. Everyone is building this.
2. **"Better dashboard"** — Visualization on data that exists elsewhere. No
   workflow integration, no lock-in, easy to copy.
3. **Platform-dependent feature** — Could be added by one platform in a quarter.
4. **Consumer social** — New social network or community platform. Winner-take-
   all dynamics, massive capital requirements, millions of users needed.
5. **Heavily regulated markets** — Healthcare billing, financial compliance,
   legal practice management — liability exposure is company-ending without
   deep regulatory expertise.
6. **Race to zero** — Commoditized service where the only differentiator is price.
7. **Requires changing behavior fundamentally** — If users must completely
   relearn how they work, adoption will be near-zero in B2B.
8. **Two-sided marketplace from scratch** — Requires solving supply AND demand
   simultaneously with no existing network.
9. **Enterprise-only TAM** — Smallest viable customer is Fortune 500. 12–18
   month sales cycle, $50K+ CAC.
10. **Incumbent feature add-on** — Makes an existing tool slightly better.
    No standalone value, easily acquired or blocked.

## Quick rejection checklist

- [ ] Not a general AI assistant
- [ ] Not just visualization/reporting
- [ ] Doesn't depend on one platform not adding it
- [ ] Not a new consumer social network
- [ ] Doesn't require regulatory expertise you lack
- [ ] Can compete on value, not price
- [ ] Doesn't require users to relearn their job
- [ ] Don't need to build supply AND demand from zero
- [ ] First customer can be small or mid-market
- [ ] Has standalone value without incumbent cooperation
