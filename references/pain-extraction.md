# Pain Extraction Guide

## Pain hierarchy (weakest to strongest)

1. **Vague dissatisfaction** — "the software isn't great" → weak, don't count
2. **Feature complaint** — "I wish it had X" → weak, incumbents can add this
3. **Workflow gap** — "I have to go to three places to do one thing" → medium
4. **Recurring manual work** — "I do this same thing every [period]" → strong
5. **Workaround with cost** — "I built a spreadsheet to track because the
   software can't" → strong
6. **Financial loss or risk** — "it cost us $X" or "we could get sued if..." → very strong
7. **Near-miss or incident** — "last month we almost lost a client because..." → very strong

## What counts as a real workaround

A workaround is real when the person has:
- Built a separate tracking system (spreadsheet, doc, sticky notes)
- Hired or assigned a person specifically to manage the gap
- Written a script or hack to bridge two systems
- Established a recurring manual ritual (weekly email, calendar reminder, copy-paste routine)

## Types of pain worth pursuing

- **Time sink:** Recurring task that takes far longer than it should
- **Coordination:** Getting information or approval from multiple people
- **Compliance/risk:** Fear of what happens if something is missed
- **Visibility:** Not knowing the status of something important
- **Collection:** Chasing people or documents repeatedly

## What NOT to count as signal

- "It would be nice if..." (aspiration, not pain)
- "I heard someone else complains about..." (secondhand, unverified)
- Pain that happens once a year (too infrequent)
- Pain that already has an affordable, well-adopted solution

## Best interview probes for real pain

- "Walk me through the last time [X] went wrong — what actually happened?"
- "What's the most annoying thing you do every week that you wish you didn't?"
- "What do you track in a spreadsheet that should be in your main tool?"
- "When was the last time this cost you real money or a real relationship?"
- "What would you do with an extra 5 hours per week?"
