# Validation Playbook

## The four-step validation ladder

Work through in order. Do not skip steps.

### Step 1 — Conversation validation (weeks 1–4)
10–15 interviews. Pass when: 80%+ describe the same pain unprompted, 3+ give
unprompted WTP signals, 2+ say "I've looked for something that does this."

### Step 2 — Public signal test (overlaps with Step 1)
Post in relevant communities describing the problem, not the solution.
Pass: 5+ comments from strangers describing the same pain.

### Step 3 — Landing page test (after Step 1 passes)
1-page site: problem + outcome, no product details. Waitlist signup.
Drive traffic via Reddit/LinkedIn post or $200 in targeted ads.
Pass: 5–10% conversion from visit to email signup.

### Step 4 — Paid commitment test (before building)
Ask 3–5 interviewees to pre-pay $100–$500 for early access.
"I want 5 design partners who pay a small amount to hold their spot and
get input on what I build."
Pass: at least 3 people pay.

## When to stop validating and start building

- 3+ people have paid money
- You can name 10 more people who fit the persona exactly
- You can describe the minimum viable version in 2–3 sentences
- You are willing to talk to customers every week for 2 years

## Common mistakes

- Asking "would you use this?" instead of "would you pay for this?"
- Counting interest ("sounds cool!") — only count commitments
- Building before getting 3 paying customers
